Rocks

Rock is a consolidated aggregate of one or more minerals formed by natural processes. In addition to minerals, rocks can also contain organic matter, fossils, water, volcanic glass, and other natural solid components.

Rocks are formed in the most varied environments on planet Earth, on the Moon, or even on other planets in our Solar System or outside it, that is, where you have the right ingredients and conditions, rocks will be formed. This consolidated natural aggregate represents the fundamental unit that makes up the earth's crust and mantle and records the geological processes that occurred in the geological past, and its study allows us to understand the evolution of our planet.

The identification of each type of rock is based on the characterization of its composition (constituent minerals), texture (size, shape and arrangement of minerals on a submillimeter to centimeter scale) and structure (contrasts in the mineralogical arrangement and/or physical discontinuities on a centimeter scale the metric).

Rocks are subdivided into 3 major groups according to their formation: igneous, sedimentary and metamorphic.

Igneous rocks are crystalline solids formed directly by the cooling and solidification of magma. Magma is the result of the partial melting (melting) of rocks inside the planet. High temperatures are required to melt a rock, usually between 700 and 1200 Â°C.

Sedimentary rocks are formed by sediments (pebbles, sand, silt and clay), or even plant and animal remains, deposited in layers over time, either on land or at the bottom of oceans, seas, rivers and lakes. Sediments are formed by the weathering process (fragmentation/decomposition) and erosion (transport) of exposed rocks on the Earth's surface, and also by the precipitation of ions in aqueous solution.

Metamorphic rocks are formed by the transformation of other rocks, with the recrystallization and/or rearrangement of minerals, due to extreme changes in pressure and temperature conditions, in general, associated with the movement of tectonic plates and the formation of mountain ranges.
