Fossils

Fossils can be the remains or just traces of organisms that by different processes were preserved in rocks or, more rarely, in another substrate such as ice or bitumen.

Fossils include a wide variety of macroscopic parts such as bones, scales, shells, carapaces, teeth, eggs, footprints, trunks, leaves, fruits, seeds and resins, and microscopic parts such as denticles, spines, spicules, sclerites, testa, frustules, teak, pollen, spores, cysts, carbonized bacteria, etc.

It is not uncommon for soft or flexible tissue remains, not organically mineralized with calcite or silica, such as muscles, cartilage, skin, veins, organs and chitin shells, to have been preserved under special conditions, as in the case of inclusion in amber, in ice or bitumen, replaced or covered by a carbonate or ferruginous crust.

The traces, technically called ichnofossils, do not include the structural parts that commonly make up an organism, but marks of activities or presence in the environment where the rocks formed. They are footprints, tracks, excavations, perforations, excrement, uroliths, resting and feeding marks, bone or shell molds, stromatolites, etc. Like the remains, the marks of activity or the presence of an organism need to be covered and protected, or they will be destroyed and disappear.

Chemical evidence called molecular fossils or biomarkers have been used to determine the presence of organic remains and, therefore, biological activity in the geological past. These signals are useful as paleoenvironmental and paleoclimatic indicators, in the determination of physical and chemical parameters such as paleotemperature or paleosalinity. Biomarkers include substances derived from biological activity and can be assigned to specific organisms. For example, the 13C/12C carbon isotope ratio indicates the presence or absence of life during the deposition time of a given sedimentary rock; the presence of styrenes indicates the activity of eukaryotic organisms; the ratio of 18O/16O isotopes in foraminifera testa is used in the determination of paleotemperature etc. Oil is an example of a very common chemical fossil.
